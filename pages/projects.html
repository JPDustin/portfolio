Posters and Flyer Creation

In my journey to continually grow as a developer and designer, I decided to dive into the world of scalable vector graphics (SVGs) using Inkscape. SVGs are an essential tool in modern web design, providing crisp, scalable images that maintain their quality at any size, making them ideal for responsive design.

Learning how to create and manipulate SVGs in Inkscape has opened up new creative possibilities for me. Unlike raster images, SVGs are defined by XML code, which means they can be styled, animated, and manipulated directly in code, offering a level of flexibility that’s hard to achieve with other image formats.

With Inkscape, I’ve been able to design custom icons, logos, and illustrations that seamlessly integrate into web projects, ensuring that the visuals are always sharp, regardless of the screen resolution. This has not only enhanced the visual appeal of my projects but also improved their performance by reducing the need for multiple image files.

The process of learning Inkscape has also deepened my understanding of vector graphics and their role in digital design. I’ve become more proficient in using layers, paths, and transformations to create complex designs, and I’m excited to continue exploring advanced features like SVG filters and animations. As I continue on this path, I’m eager to see how I can further incorporate SVGs into my work to create even more dynamic and engaging web experiences.

Mobile App Development

I currently drive for UPS, and like many of my colleagues, I keep track of my work hours on paper. While this method is straightforward, it can become cumbersome when I need to go back through the records to find specific details or quickly query data. This experience made me realize how inefficient paper tracking can be, especially when accurate records are crucial for ensuring fair compensation and adherence to our work contract.

Motivated by this challenge, I began developing a simplified tracking app tailored specifically to our needs as UPS drivers. This app is designed to align with the unique aspects of our contract, allowing us to easily track our hours worked, calculate specific metrics related to overtime, bonuses, and other contractual obligations, and query our data whenever necessary.

The app aims to replace the manual, time-consuming process of paper tracking with a more efficient, digital solution that not only simplifies record-keeping but also empowers drivers to stay informed about their work hours and earnings. By creating a tool that meets our specific needs, I hope to make the day-to-day operations smoother for myself and my fellow drivers, providing us with greater control and insight into our work schedules and compensation.

Once I finish the iOS version of this app, I plan on developing the Android version to allow everyone to have access to the app.
